What Does Army MOS 25B Translate to in Civilian Jobs?

If you served as an Army 25B Information Technology Specialist, you are sitting on one of the most directly transferable military skill sets in the entire Department of Defense. Unlike many MOS codes that require heavy translation for civilian hiring managers, 25B maps almost one-to-one to some of the highest-demand, highest-paying careers in the civilian economy. You installed, configured, and maintained computer systems, networks, and communications equipment that supported entire battalions and brigades. In the civilian world, that is called being an IT professional — and companies are desperate to hire people who can do what you already know how to do.

In civilian terms, a 25B IT Specialist translates to roles like IT Support Specialist, Cybersecurity Analyst, Network Administrator, Systems Administrator, and Cloud Engineer. The technology sector has a massive talent shortage, with over 750,000 unfilled cybersecurity positions in the United States alone. Your military training on SIPR/NIPR networks, GCSS-Army, Active Directory, and DoD security protocols gives you a foundation that civilian IT boot camp graduates spend $15,000 or more trying to replicate — and they still lack the real-world experience you built under operational pressure.

The average 25B veteran who earns even one industry certification (CompTIA Security+ or AWS Solutions Architect) within 6 months of separation lands a role paying $65,000 to $95,000. Veterans who stack certifications and target the DC metro area, San Antonio, or Colorado Springs — where defense contractors cluster — routinely break $100,000 within 18 months of ETS.

Top 5 Civilian Career Paths for 25B IT Specialist Veterans

1. IT Support Specialist ($45,000 - $75,000)

IT Support Specialists serve as the first line of technical troubleshooting for organizations, handling help desk tickets, hardware and software issues, user account management, and system configuration. As a 25B, you have been doing this exact work for years — except your "users" were soldiers in field conditions with zero tolerance for downtime. Your experience troubleshooting systems in austere environments, often without vendor support or internet access, makes you far more resourceful than most civilian help desk candidates. This is often a stepping stone role that leads to network administration or systems engineering within 12 to 24 months. Companies like Leidos, SAIC, ManTech, and Booz Allen Hamilton hire directly from the 25B pipeline for Tier 1 and Tier 2 support positions, especially for roles requiring a security clearance.

2. Cybersecurity Analyst ($70,000 - $120,000)

Cybersecurity Analysts monitor networks for security breaches, investigate incidents, implement security protocols, and ensure compliance with regulatory frameworks. Your 25B training in Information Assurance (IA), network security, STIG compliance, and managing classified systems is exactly what this role demands. The cybersecurity field is projected to grow 32% through 2032, making it one of the fastest-growing career paths in the entire economy. With a CompTIA Security+ (which many 25Bs already hold from military requirements) and your hands-on experience, you can enter at the mid-level and bypass the entry-level grind that civilian candidates face. Defense contractors and federal agencies (NSA, DHS CISA, DISA) specifically target veterans with 25B backgrounds for SOC analyst and vulnerability assessment roles.

3. Network Administrator ($55,000 - $95,000)

Network Administrators design, install, and maintain an organization's computer networks, including LANs, WANs, intranets, and VPNs. If you configured routers, switches, and firewalls for battalion or brigade tactical networks, you have direct experience in this field. Civilian network admin work involves the same Cisco, Juniper, and Palo Alto equipment you used in the military, just without the tactical fiber and OE-254 antennas. A Cisco CCNA certification combined with your military networking experience positions you competitively for roles at managed service providers, healthcare systems, financial institutions, and government agencies. Network administrators in major metro areas regularly earn $80,000 to $95,000.

4. Systems Administrator ($60,000 - $100,000)

Systems Administrators manage and maintain the servers, operating systems, and enterprise applications that keep businesses running. Your 25B experience with Windows Server, Active Directory, Group Policy, SCCM, and Exchange Server translates directly. The military taught you to manage systems at scale — supporting hundreds or thousands of user accounts, maintaining 99.9% uptime requirements, and implementing security patches on aggressive timelines. In the civilian world, companies pay $75,000 to $100,000 for sysadmins who can do exactly that. The jump from sysadmin to DevOps Engineer or Cloud Architect is a natural progression that can push your salary above $130,000 within 3 to 5 years.

5. Cloud Engineer ($80,000 - $140,000)

Cloud Engineers design, deploy, and manage cloud infrastructure on platforms like AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud. The Department of Defense is in the middle of a massive cloud migration, and if you touched any cloud-based systems during your service, you have a head start. Even without direct cloud experience, your foundational knowledge of networking, virtualization, and systems administration maps directly to cloud concepts. AWS offers a free Military Cloud Skills program, and Microsoft has the MSSA (Microsoft Software and Systems Academy) specifically for transitioning service members. An AWS Solutions Architect Associate or Azure Administrator certification combined with your 25B background can land you a cloud role paying $90,000 to $140,000, especially in tech hubs and defense corridors.

Average Salaries for 25B Veterans by Role and Location

IT salaries vary significantly by location, clearance status, and certification stack. Veterans with an active TS/SCI clearance can add $20,000 to $50,000 to the ranges below when working for defense contractors or federal agencies.

  • IT Support Specialist: $45,000 (rural, entry) to $75,000 (metro, cleared). National median: $58,000.
  • Cybersecurity Analyst: $70,000 (mid-market) to $120,000 (DC, cleared). Senior SOC analysts in Northern Virginia earn $100,000-$120,000.
  • Network Administrator: $55,000 (small company) to $95,000 (enterprise, metro). Cleared net admins in San Antonio average $82,000.
  • Systems Administrator: $60,000 (entry, mid-market) to $100,000 (senior, metro). Federal GS-12 sysadmins in DC earn $90,000+ with locality.
  • Cloud Engineer: $80,000 (entry) to $140,000 (senior, FAANG-adjacent). AWS and Azure specialists in Seattle, Austin, and DC command the highest rates.

Required Certifications for 25B Career Transition

The IT industry is certification-driven, and your military training gives you a massive head start. Many 25Bs already hold Security+ from their DoD 8570/8140 requirements. Here are the certifications that will accelerate your transition:

  • CompTIA A+: The entry-level IT certification. If you do not already have it, it validates your hardware and software troubleshooting skills. Many 25Bs skip this and go straight to Network+ or Security+, but it is useful for help desk and desktop support roles.
  • CompTIA Network+: Validates your networking knowledge — subnetting, routing, switching, network security. Directly relevant to network administrator roles. Cost: approximately $370; DoD COOL covers it while on active duty.
  • CompTIA Security+: The single most valuable entry-level certification for 25B veterans. Required for all DoD IAT Level II positions, which means every defense contractor IT role requires it. If you do not have it yet, get it before you separate. It opens the door to cybersecurity analyst roles paying $70,000+.
  • Cisco CCNA: The gold standard for network administration. Validates your ability to configure and manage Cisco routers and switches — the same equipment you worked with in the Army. Significantly boosts salary for networking roles.
  • AWS Solutions Architect Associate: The most in-demand cloud certification. Proves you can design distributed systems on AWS. Combined with your military systems experience, this certification alone can land you a cloud role paying $90,000+. AWS offers free training for military members.

Resume Tips Specifically for 25B IT Specialist Veterans

Your 25B resume needs to speak the language of civilian IT hiring managers and, more importantly, pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) that scan for specific keywords. Here is how to optimize it:

  • List specific systems and platforms: Name the exact tools you used — GCSS-Army, SIPR/NIPR, Active Directory, Windows Server 2016/2019, Cisco IOS, SCCM, Exchange Server, VMware. These are the keywords ATS systems scan for.
  • Quantify your network scope: "Administered network infrastructure supporting 500+ users across 3 sites with 99.7% uptime" is infinitely more powerful than "maintained network equipment."
  • Highlight your clearance: Put your clearance level (Secret, Top Secret, TS/SCI) near the top of your resume. In the defense contracting world, this is often the first thing recruiters look for.
  • List certifications prominently: Create a dedicated "Certifications" section near the top. CompTIA Security+, Network+, CCNA, AWS certs — these are the first filters IT recruiters apply.
  • Translate military systems to civilian equivalents: "Managed Army SIPR network" becomes "Administered classified network infrastructure with strict security compliance requirements." The technical skills are the same; only the vocabulary changes.

25B Veteran Success Story

Specialist Daniels served 5 years as a 25B at Fort Hood, supporting a brigade combat team of 4,000 soldiers. She held a Security+ certification from her military requirements and managed the S6 shop's SIPR and NIPR networks. During her last 6 months of service, she used SkillBridge to intern at Microsoft's Military Affairs program, where she earned her Azure Administrator certification for free. She used DoD COOL to add her CCNA before separation. Within 45 days of ETS, Daniels was hired as a Network Security Engineer at a defense contractor in San Antonio, starting at $87,000 with her Secret clearance. One year later, she earned her AWS Solutions Architect certification and was promoted to Senior Cloud Security Engineer at $108,000. Her advice to 25B veterans: "You already know more than you think. Get your certs stacked before you leave, use SkillBridge, and target defense contractors in San Antonio, DC, or Colorado Springs — they are literally fighting over cleared IT veterans."

Resume Translation: Before & After

The biggest mistake 25B veterans make on resumes is using military language civilian hiring managers don't understand.

❌ Wrong Way
"Served as 25B in support of unit operations IAW applicable regulations and directives."
✅ Right Way
"Led a team of [X] personnel executing [function] in support of [Y-person organization]; achieved [result] while maintaining [standard]."
❌ Wrong Way
"Responsible for the maintenance and accountability of assigned equipment."
✅ Right Way
"Maintained accountability for $[X] in equipment across [Y] items with zero losses or discrepancies over [time period]."
