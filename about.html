About Veteran Career Path

Built by a medically retired U.S. Army Sergeant who lived the transition struggle — and decided to build something better for every veteran who comes after.

My Story

My name is Ruben Mendoza. I am a medically retired U.S. Army E-5 Sergeant who served as an 11B Infantryman. When I separated from the Army, I thought the hardest part of my career was behind me. I was wrong. The transition from military to civilian life hit me harder than any field exercise, deployment, or training event I had ever been through.

The resume was the first problem. I had years of leadership experience, mission planning under pressure, personnel accountability, and real-world operational execution — but none of that translated to a civilian resume. I did not know how to explain what an infantryman does to a hiring manager who has never heard of an MOS code. I could not figure out which certifications I needed, which benefits I had actually earned, or which civilian industries would value what I brought to the table.

TAP gave me a three-day class and a handshake. It was not enough. Not even close. I spent months trying to piece together information from dozens of government websites, outdated pamphlets, and well-meaning but unhelpful advice from people who had never worn the uniform. I watched other veterans — people who had served with distinction — struggle with the same exact problems. Good people with real skills, stuck because the civilian world does not speak military.

That experience is why I built VeteranCareerPath.com. Not as a side project or a hobby — as a mission. The same way we executed missions in the Army, I set out to build the most comprehensive, most accessible, and most useful resource that any veteran could find online. One place where everything you need is explained in plain language, organized by what actually matters, and available without jumping through hoops.

Every one of these resources is completely free. No account required. No credit card. No paywall. I wanted this information accessible to every veteran, military spouse, and transitioning service member — including the ones who cannot afford to pay for career coaching.

The AI Tools

For veterans who want more personalized, hands-on career support, Veteran Career Path also offers 20+ AI-powered career tools for $15 per month. These are not generic chatbots. Every tool pre-loads your military profile — MOS, branch, rank, clearance level, and duties — so you never have to start from scratch or explain what your service means.

The AI tools include a resume builder with ATS optimization, a real MOS experience translator, a job match analyzer that scores you against any job posting, a federal resume builder for USAJobs applications, an interview simulator with STAR-format coaching, a salary negotiation advisor, a LinkedIn profile optimizer, a certification advisor, a VA claim builder, and more. Each tool was built specifically for veterans because I know firsthand that generic career tools do not work for us.

The Mission

The mission of Veteran Career Path is simple: close the gap between military service and civilian success. Every veteran who separates from the military has earned skills, benefits, and opportunities that they should be able to access without confusion, without gatekeeping, and without spending thousands of dollars on consultants.

I am one veteran building this platform, and I am committed to making it the single best resource available. If Veteran Career Path helps even one service member avoid the confusion and frustration I went through, then this mission is worth it. But I am building it to help hundreds of thousands.

Thank you for your service. Now let me help you with your next mission.

— Ruben Mendoza, U.S. Army (Ret.), E-5 Sergeant, 11B Infantryman
