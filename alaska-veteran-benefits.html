Alaska Veteran Benefits

Complete 2026 guide to veteran benefits in Alaska — home to Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson and Eielson AFB communities.

🏠 Property Tax Benefits

100% Disabled Veterans

100% P&T disabled veterans may qualify for a full property tax exemption on their primary residence. Contact your county assessor to apply.

Partial Disability Exemption

Veterans with 10%+ VA disability ratings typically qualify for partial property tax exemptions. The exemption amount increases with disability rating.

Surviving Spouse

Unremarried surviving spouses of veterans killed in action or who died from service-connected disabilities often qualify for property tax relief.

🎓 Education Benefits

In-State Tuition

Veterans using GI Bill benefits at Alaska public universities qualify for in-state tuition rates regardless of residency period, per the Veterans Access, Choice and Accountability Act.

State Scholarship Programs

Alaska may offer state-funded scholarships for children and spouses of veterans killed or totally disabled in service. Check with the Alaska DVA for current programs.

National Guard Tuition

Active Alaska National Guard members typically qualify for reduced or waived tuition at state universities. Contact your unit education officer.

🎣 Recreation & License Benefits

Hunting & Fishing Licenses

100% disabled veterans in Alaska typically qualify for free or reduced-cost hunting and fishing licenses. Check with Alaska Fish & Wildlife for current rates.

State Park Passes

Many Alaska state parks offer free or discounted admission for veterans and active duty service members. Some require a veteran ID or DD-214.

Vehicle Registration

Veterans in Alaska may qualify for free or discounted vehicle registration, specialty veteran license plates, and disabled veteran plates with parking privileges.

🏥 Healthcare & VA Resources

Alaska DVA Office

The Alaska Department of Veterans Affairs administers state benefits, accredits VSOs, and provides claims assistance. Capital: Juneau.

TRICARE & VA Healthcare

Federal VA healthcare is available to eligible veterans at VA medical centers and community-based outpatient clinics throughout Alaska.

Mental Health Resources

Veterans Crisis Line: 988, press 1. The Alaska DVA also offers state-funded mental health programs for veterans not eligible for VA care.
