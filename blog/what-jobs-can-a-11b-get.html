11B Infantry: Civilian Careers That Actually Value Combat Experience

By Veteran Career Path · March 28, 2026 · 9 min read

Being 11B is the most common MOS in the Army — and it's also one of the most misunderstood by civilian employers. Your combat experience, leadership under fire, and ability to make decisions with incomplete information are worth far more than most job postings let on.

The Truth About 11B Civilian Value

Recruiters who don't understand military service will see "Infantry" and think "limited transferable skills." They're wrong. Here's what 11B actually means to the right employer.

High-Value Career Paths

Law Enforcement (Best Direct Translation)

Your weapons proficiency, rules of engagement understanding, and ability to de-escalate make you an ideal police candidate. Many departments are specifically recruiting veterans to address staffing shortages.

  • Local/state police: $45,000–$85,000 starting, pension included
  • Federal agents (FBI, ATF, Secret Service): $55,000–$75,000 starting
  • Border Patrol: $52,000–$70,000 with rapid advancement
  • Military Police contractor overseas: $80,000–$150,000

Security Management

Corporate security, executive protection, and security consulting are booming industries. 11B experience with cleared DOD contractors can command $90,000–$130,000.

Operations and Logistics Management

Every infantry unit runs logistics. Your experience managing equipment, personnel, and resources under pressure translates directly to operations management roles.

💡 Clearance Leverage

If you held a Secret or TS clearance as an 11B, that's worth $15,000–$40,000 in additional salary in the right roles. Don't leave it off your resume.

How to Write Your 11B Experience for Civilians

Never write "trained in combat operations." Instead write: "Led a 9-person team in high-stakes operational environments, managing real-time decision-making, personnel safety, and mission execution with zero margin for error."
