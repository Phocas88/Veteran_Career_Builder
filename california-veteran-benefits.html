🌴 State Benefits

California Veteran Benefits Guide

CalVet home loans, up to $237K property tax exemption for 100% disabled veterans, free UC/CSU tuition for qualifying dependents. Important: California taxes military retirement pay — plan accordingly.

View All State Benefits

California has the largest veteran population of any state and significant veteran-specific benefits — though high state income taxes and cost of living mean careful planning is needed to maximize your overall financial picture. These benefits require separate application and are independent of federal VA benefits.

Property Tax Benefits

The California Veterans Exemption provides a property tax exemption up to $4,000 of assessed value for veterans who served during a period of war and owned property on January 1 of the tax year. The Disabled Veterans Exemption is significantly more valuable:

Disability StatusExemption (2025 approx.)
100% disabled veteran (basic)$196,262 of assessed value
100% disabled veteran (low income)$236,998 of assessed value (income below ~$60,000)
Surviving spouse of 100% disabled veteranSame as disabled veteran exemption

On a $800,000 home assessed at $500,000 (Prop 13), the $196,262 exemption reduces taxable value to ~$303,738 — saving approximately $2,300-$3,000/year in property taxes depending on local rates.

State Income Tax on Military Retirement

California is one of the least tax-friendly states for military retirees. California taxes military retirement pay as ordinary income at rates up to 13.3%. VA disability compensation is exempt from California income tax (federal law prohibits state taxation of VA disability). Active duty pay for service performed outside California is exempt. Many military retirees choose not to establish California domicile specifically to avoid military retirement taxation.

CalVet Home Loan Program

The California Department of Veterans Affairs (CalVet) offers home loan financing through the Cal-Vet Home Loan program. Benefits include competitive interest rates (often below market), no prepayment penalty, and inclusion of a master insurance policy at group rates covering fire, earthquake, and life insurance. Available to veterans who are California residents or were California residents at the time of entry into service.

Education Benefits

The Cal Vet Fee Waiver provides free tuition at California State University (CSU) and University of California (UC) campuses for children of veterans who are totally disabled due to service-connected causes or who died in service. The waiver covers enrollment fees, non-resident tuition, student body fees, and campus fees. Contact the California Department of Veterans Affairs at calvet.ca.gov.

Other Key Benefits

California Department of Veterans Affairs (CalVet)

Free claims assistance, advocacy, and benefits navigation. calvet.ca.gov — county veterans service offices statewide.

Free Help
Veterans Homes of California

Eight state veterans homes providing residential care. Priority for wartime veterans. Fees are income-based and significantly subsidized.

License and Certification Fee Waivers

Many California professional licenses waive fees for veterans. Department of Consumer Affairs (dca.ca.gov) lists waivers by profession.

State Employment Veterans Preference

California state government positions give 5-point (or 10-point for disabled veterans) preference in civil service examinations. Applies to state agencies, not local government.
