DD-214 Decoder

Every field on your DD-214 explained in plain English - what it means for VA benefits, federal employment, and your civilian career.

Use the MOS Translator

Your DD-214 is the single most important document you carry out of the military. Every civilian employer, VA office, federal agency, and VSO will ask for it. Most veterans don't fully understand what's on it or how to use it effectively.

Most Common Mistake

Veterans submit their DD-214 to employers without understanding what it says. Know your document before you hand it to anyone.

Field-by-Field Breakdown

Block 4a/4b - Grade, Rate, Rank and Pay Grade

Your rank at separation. Always translate this on civilian resumes. E-7 becomes "Sergeant First Class - led teams of 20-40 personnel." A Sergeant Major managed 100-200+ personnel with multi-million dollar equipment accountability. Quantify it explicitly.

Block 11 - Primary Specialty (MOS/Rate/AFSC)

The field civilians will try to research but often can't understand. Supplement every application with a civilian translation. Use our MOS Translator to generate the equivalent.

Block 12 - Record of Service

Net active service, total service. Most VA benefits require 24 months of continuous active service for post-9/11 era veterans. Civilian employers focus on total years of service.

Block 13 - Decorations, Medals and Awards

Translate every award. Army Commendation Medal means formally recognized by senior command for superior performance. Combat Infantryman Badge means performed under direct enemy fire. Purple Heart means wounded in combat - you choose whether to include this on civilian applications.

Federal hiring tip: Purple Heart recipients receive 10-point veterans preference regardless of disability rating. List all awards on federal applications - it matters.

Block 14 - Military Education

Heavily underutilized. Military schools have civilian credit equivalencies through ACE (American Council on Education). Warrior Leader Course, Advanced NCO Course, and officer professional military education all carry college credit recommendations. Check the ACE Military Guide for your specific schools.

Block 23/24 - Type and Character of Separation

The most critical fields for VA benefits and federal employment. "Honorable" opens all doors. "General Under Honorable Conditions" qualifies for most but not all VA benefits. If you received anything other than Honorable and believe it was unjust, a character upgrade is possible through the Discharge Review Board - thousands of veterans have successfully upgraded their discharge.

Block 26 - Separation Code

A letter-number code explaining the specific reason for separation. Standard end-of-service codes (JBK, JCC, LBK) are unremarkable. If yours is non-standard, research it before federal applications - certain codes flag in background investigations.

Block 28 - Narrative Reason for Separation

"Completion of Required Active Service" is the cleanest outcome. Disability-related separations here actually support VA claims. Research your specific code if anything other than standard applies.

What Civilian Employers Actually Look At

Block 24 - Character of Service

"Honorable" is what HR departments verify first. This single field opens or closes civilian employment doors.

Block 12 - Years of Service

Total active service. Each year above 4 signals commitment and stability to civilian employers unfamiliar with military culture.

Block 11 - MOS/Rate/AFSC

Have the civilian translation ready. Never assume an employer will research what your MOS means.

Block 13 - Awards

Leadership awards (MSM, ARCOM, NAM) signal recognized performance. Translate every one into civilian language on your resume.

Using Your DD-214 for Federal Employment

Veterans preference gives eligible veterans a significant advantage in federal hiring. Five-point preference requires an honorable discharge after at least 181 days of continuous active service. Ten-point preference requires a service-connected disability of 10%+ or certain awards including the Purple Heart. Always apply with the correct preference - federal agencies are required to honor it, and many hiring managers actively recruit veterans with 10-point preference because it simplifies their hiring process.

Storing and Replacing Your DD-214

Store certified copies in multiple locations - physical and digital. If you need a replacement, request it through the National Archives eVetRecs system at archives.gov/veterans. Allow 2-4 weeks for standard requests; expedited copies are available for urgent VA claims. Minimum recommended: one original, two certified copies, and an encrypted digital backup.
