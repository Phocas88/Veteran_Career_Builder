The Translation Problem Every Veteran Faces

You spent years mastering your craft. As an 88M Motor Transport Operator, you managed convoys, maintained accountability for millions of dollars of equipment, coordinated logistics across multiple units, trained junior soldiers, and ensured mission success under pressure. Common MOS Translations

11B Infantryman to Civilian Careers

The Infantryman is the most common transition challenge. Civilian employers see "combat arms" and do not know what to do with it. Our translator converts 11B experience into leadership, team management, security, law enforcement, operations management, and training roles. An experienced 11B Staff Sergeant translates to a team leader or operations supervisor with demonstrated crisis management and personnel development experience.

68W Combat Medic to Civilian Careers

The 68W has some of the clearest civilian translation paths: EMT, paramedic, medical assistant, surgical technician, healthcare administrator, and clinical coordinator. With additional education, the 68W becomes a registered nurse, physician assistant, or healthcare manager. The VA and DoD health systems actively recruit 68W veterans for their real-world experience.

25B IT Specialist to Civilian Careers

Army 25B, Navy IT, and Air Force 3D1X1 all translate directly to civilian IT roles: network administrator, systems administrator, cybersecurity analyst, IT project manager, and cloud engineer. With a CompTIA Security Plus or AWS certification, veterans in these fields regularly land six-figure salaries within 12 months of separation.

35F Intelligence Analyst to Civilian Careers

The intelligence community, defense contractors, financial institutions, consulting firms, and government agencies all actively recruit 35F veterans. Civilian titles include intelligence analyst, threat analyst, corporate intelligence specialist, data analyst, and program manager. A TS/SCI clearance adds significant salary premium to any of these paths.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this work for all military branches?

Yes. We support Army MOS, Navy NEC and Rate codes, Marine Corps MOS, Air Force AFSC, Coast Guard ratings, and Space Force AFSC codes. All six branches are fully supported.

What if I had multiple MOS codes or cross-training?

You can enter multiple military experiences in your profile, each with its own MOS, duties, and qualifications. The AI builds your resume to reflect the full breadth of your service.

Does it include my additional duties and qualifications?

Yes. The system captures your ASIs, SQIs, NECs, SEIs, collateral duties (SHARP rep, unit armorer, retention NCO), and sub-roles held within your MOS. Every qualification you earned is included in your civilian translation.

What about my security clearance?

Your clearance level is included in your profile and factored into career recommendations. You can choose whether to include it on your resume.
