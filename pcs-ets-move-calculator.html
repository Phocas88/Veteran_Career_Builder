What Counts as Pro-Gear?

Pro-gear (professional gear) is weight that doesn't count against your HHG entitlement. It must be items required for your military job or your spouse's career.

✅ Counts as Pro-GearBooks, papers, and equipment required for your duties. Musical instruments for a professional musician spouse. Tools and equipment for technical trades used professionally.
⚠️ Does NOT Count as Pro-GearRecreational equipment (golf clubs, fishing gear, guns for sport). Exercise equipment. Hobby items. Collections.

Unaccompanied Baggage (UB)

You can ship up to 1,000 lbs (with dependents) or 600 lbs (without) as unaccompanied baggage — this moves by air or express freight and arrives at your destination before your HHG. The weight comes out of your total HHG entitlement.

Temporary Lodging Expense (TLE) — CONUSTLE reimburses lodging and meal expenses when you can't occupy permanent quarters immediately before or after a PCS move within the continental U.S.

TLA — Overseas (OCONUS)

Temporary Lodging Allowance (TLA)For OCONUS moves, TLA applies instead of TLE. TLA is paid at 100% of per diem for the first 30 days, then reduces. Maximum duration: 60 days (up to 90 days with command approval in some locations). Specific rates set by SECDEF for each OCONUS location — check current DTMO tables.

Claiming TLE

  • File DD Form 1351-2 (Travel Voucher) with all receipts
  • Include lodging receipts and copy of PCS orders
  • Can file within 5 business days of completing move for advance pay or afterward for reimbursement
  • TLE at gaining installation requires finance office coordination
DITY / PPM (Personally Procured Move) — How to Make Money on Your MoveInstead of using the government movers, you handle your own move and get paid 95% of what the government would have paid a commercial carrier. If you move cheaper than that, you keep the difference as profit.

How PPM Incentive Pay Works

StepWhat Happens
1. Request PPMContact your Transportation Office (TO) and request PPM authorization before moving. Do NOT move before authorization.
2. Get Weight TicketsWeigh your truck/trailer empty (tare weight) at a certified scale BEFORE loading. Then weigh again full (gross weight). Difference = net weight shipped.
3. Government Calculates RateGovernment determines what a commercial carrier would have charged for that weight and distance. You receive 95% of that amount.
4. You Keep the ProfitIf your actual moving costs were less than the 95% payment, you keep the difference — TAX-FREE up to the authorized entitlement.
5. Submit PaperworkSubmit weight tickets, receipts, and PPM packet to Finance within 45 days of arriving at new duty station.

PPM Rate Formula (2026)

Incentive Pay = Weight Shipped × Government Rate Per Pound × Distance × 95% Current government rate averages $0.12–$0.22 per pound per 100 miles depending on origin/destination. Your Transportation Office will give you the exact rate for your specific move.

PPM Tips — Maximize Your Profit

  • Rent a truck, don't hire movers. U-Haul or Penske costs $200–$800 vs. a government move worth $3,000–$10,000+
  • Ship your car separately. POV shipment is a separate entitlement — don't mix it with PPM weight
  • Get multiple weight tickets. If you make multiple loads, weigh each separately
  • Keep ALL receipts. Gas, tolls, truck rental, boxes, packing materials — all deductible
  • PPM profit is NOT taxed if within entitlement. Money above your entitlement weight IS taxable
  • DITY advance. You can get 60% of estimated PPM payment in advance — ask your TO

Partial DITY

You can do a partial DITY — ship some items yourself and let the government ship the rest. Many service members ship small/valuable items themselves and have the government handle furniture. You get PPM pay on your self-moved weight.

Critical PCS Rules Everyone Should Know

⚠️ Do NOT Move Before Orders Are CutMoving before receiving official PCS orders means you pay for everything yourself. "Verbal orders" and "email notices" are NOT sufficient. Wait for the official DA Form 4651 or equivalent branch-specific orders document.
⚠️ Storage LimitsGovernment-funded storage at destination (NTS — Non-Temporary Storage) is authorized for up to 1 year for PCS. At origin, you can store up to 90 days in temporary storage (SIT — Storage in Transit). Excess time = you pay the bill.
ℹ️ 1 Year to Use Move EntitlementYou have 1 year from your report date to use your PCS move entitlement. After 1 year, you lose it. Do not leave furniture at the old location and expect to move it a year later on the government's dime.
💡 Dependent Pickup PointIf your dependents live somewhere other than your current duty station, you may be authorized to ship HHG from the dependent's location as well as your current station — up to your total weight entitlement. Coordinate with your TO.

Special Circumstances

  • Pregnancy/Birth: If a dependent delivers during your move, you may receive additional TLE/TLA extensions
  • Short Tours: Some OCONUS short tours (unaccompanied) authorize storing HHG in CONUS at government expense for the tour duration
  • Marriage During PCS: If you marry after orders are cut but before you arrive, coordinate with your Transportation Office — dependent weight allowance may apply
  • Divorce During PCS: Immediately notify your TO and Finance — entitlements change based on court orders and timing
  • Death of Service Member: Dependents receive one final move entitlement to home of record or any CONUS location
ETS / Separation Move EntitlementsWhen you separate (ETS) or retire, you receive one final move to your Home of Record (HOR) or Place of Enlistment/Induction (PLEAD) — whichever is more beneficial. For retirement, you also have the option of moving to any CONUS location.

Separation Pay vs. Severance Pay

⚠️ Not a Moving Allowance — But You Need to KnowSeparation pay (Involuntary Separation Pay or VSP) and Severance Pay are different from moving allowances. If you receive separation pay, it may be recouped if you later receive VA disability compensation for the same period. Consult with a VA benefits advisor before separating.
