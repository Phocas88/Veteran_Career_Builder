🏛 State Benefits

State Veteran Benefits All 50 States

Property tax exemptions, income tax exclusions, education grants, home loans, and license waivers. State benefits are separate from federal VA benefits — most veterans never claim them.

View Federal Benefits Checklist

Every state offers veteran benefits beyond federal VA programs — but they are almost never automatic. You have to apply for them, often through separate agencies, and most veterans never do. This guide covers the most valuable state-level benefits across all 50 states, organized by benefit type.

These Are Not Automatic

State veteran benefits do not activate when you move to a state. You must apply through your state's Department of Veterans Affairs or equivalent agency. Most benefits require proof of honorable discharge (DD-214) and state residency. Apply as soon as you establish residency — some benefits are not retroactive.

State Home Loan Programs (In Addition to VA Loan)

Several states offer veteran-specific home loan programs that can be used alongside the VA loan or independently. Texas Veterans Land Board (VLB) offers land loans, home loans, and home improvement loans at below-market rates. California's CalVet Home Loan program offers competitive rates and streamlined process for California residents. Oregon, Wisconsin, and Mississippi also have veteran home loan programs. Check your state's Department of Veterans Affairs for current rates and eligibility.

Vehicle Registration and License Plates

Most states offer reduced or free vehicle registration for disabled veterans, specialized license plates (often free), and reserved parking permits. States with the strongest vehicle benefits include Texas (free registration for 100% disabled), Florida (one free specialty plate per vehicle, discounted registration), and Virginia (free license plate for 100% disabled). Apply through your state DMV with a copy of your VA disability letter.

Professional License Fee Waivers

Many states waive application and renewal fees for professional licenses (contractor, real estate, nursing, cosmetology, etc.) for veterans. Some states also have expedited licensing processes that accept military training as equivalent to civilian requirements. States with the most comprehensive license waivers include Texas, Florida, Virginia, and California. Check with your state's licensing board for your specific profession.

Find Your State Benefits

State DVA Websites

Every state has a Department of Veterans Affairs (or equivalent). Search "[your state] department of veterans affairs" to find your state's complete benefit listing and application processes.

NaVOBA / NACVSO

The National Association of County Veterans Service Officers (NACVSO) maintains a directory of county-level VSOs who can help you identify and apply for all state and local benefits. Free service.

Military OneSource

militaryonesource.mil has a state-by-state benefits tool that lists benefits with application instructions. Accessible to veterans up to 365 days after separation.

DAV / VFW / American Legion

Accredited claims agents at these VSOs can identify every benefit you qualify for at the federal, state, and local level. Free service, no appointment needed in most cases.

Know Every Federal Benefit You're Entitled To

State benefits are in addition to federal VA benefits. Make sure you're claiming everything at both levels.
