Rating Schedule 2026

Complete listing of all VA-rated conditions, their rating percentages, qualifying criteria, and common secondary conditions. Based on the updated Schedule for Rating Disabilities (CFR 38 Part 4).

⚠️ Important Disclaimer This is an educational reference based on CFR 38 Part 4. Actual ratings are determined by VA raters based on your specific C&P exam findings, medical evidence, and individual circumstances. These percentages represent the possible ratings — your actual rating may differ. Always file for all conditions and let the VA determine your rating.

🔥 Burn Pit & Toxic Exposure (PACT Act)

▼
💡 File Now — Don't Wait for Symptoms to WorsenUnder the PACT Act, many cancers and respiratory conditions are presumptive for veterans who served in covered locations. File early to establish the effective date — your disability pay goes back to your filing date, not when VA approves your claim.

Covered Locations for PACT Act Presumptives

Served on or after August 2, 1990 in: Afghanistan, Djibouti, Egypt, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Somalia, Syria, Uzbekistan, Yemen, Iraq, or any other location designated by the Secretary of Defense.

⚠️ The VA Does NOT Simply Add RatingsThe VA uses the "whole person" method. Your highest rating is applied first to 100% of an imaginary person. Each subsequent rating is applied to the remaining "able-bodied" portion. This always results in a combined rating lower than the sum of individual ratings.

💡 How to Increase Your Combined RatingAdding conditions is less effective at higher combined ratings due to diminishing returns. A 90% combined rating plus a new 10% = 91% (rounds to 90%).
