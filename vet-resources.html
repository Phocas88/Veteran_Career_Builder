Mental Health & Crisis Support

Veterans Crisis Line
Free, confidential support for veterans in crisis. Call 988 and press 1, text 838255, or chat online. Available 24/7. Staffed by VA-trained responders, many of whom are veterans themselves.
Call 988 → Press 1 →
Make the Connection
Real stories from veterans who have faced mental health challenges. Find local VA mental health services, peer support, and community programs across the country.
maketheconnection.net →
VA Mental Health Services
VA offers free mental health care to veterans including therapy, medication, PTSD treatment, depression, anxiety, military sexual trauma (MST) counseling, and more. You do NOT need a service-connected...
va.gov/mental-health →
Headstrong (Free Mental Health for Post-9/11 Vets)
Free, stigma-free mental health treatment for post-9/11 veterans and their families. No VA enrollment required. Works with private therapists across the country. Completely confidential.
giveanhourhq.org/headstrong →
Team Red White & Blue
Connects veterans to their community through physical and social activity. Chapters nationwide. Research shows social connection through physical activity is one of the most effective tools against ve...
teamrwb.org →
PTSD Coach App (VA)
Free app developed by VA to help veterans manage PTSD symptoms with tools, education, and self-assessments. Available on iOS and Android.
ptsd.va.gov/appvid/mobile/ptsdcoach.asp →
TAPS — Tragedy Assistance Program for Survivors
Support for families who have lost a loved one in military service. Grief counseling, peer mentors, and survivor benefits guidance.
taps.org →
VA Substance Use Disorder Treatment
Free inpatient and outpatient treatment for alcohol and drug dependency. VA treats substance use as a medical condition, not a moral failing. Call your local VA or the Veterans Crisis Line.
va.gov/health-care/health-needs-conditions/substance-use-problems →

VA Benefits & Claims

VA.gov — Benefits Hub
Official hub for all VA benefits: disability compensation, pension, education (GI Bill), home loans, life insurance, health care enrollment, and more.
va.gov →
Disabled American Veterans (DAV)
Free VSO (Veterans Service Organization) that helps veterans file and appeal VA disability claims. A DAV accredited claims agent can significantly increase your rating and get back pay you are owed.
dav.org →
VFW Veterans Benefits Center
Free VSO with accredited claims agents nationwide. They have helped veterans recover billions in retroactive disability pay. No cost ever.
vfw.org/assistance →
VA Vocational Rehabilitation (VR&E / Chapter 31)
If you have a service-connected disability, VA may pay 100% of education or job training costs, provide job placement assistance, and a monthly subsistence allowance while you train.
va.gov/careers-employment/vocational-rehabilitation →
Benefits.gov — Federal Benefits Finder
Search all federal benefits you may be eligible for based on your situation — veterans, family, low income, disability, housing and more.
benefits.gov →
VA Pension — Low Income Veterans
If you served during wartime and have limited income, you may qualify for VA Pension — a monthly payment regardless of disability rating.
va.gov/pension →
GI Bill (Chapter 33 / Post-9/11)
Covers tuition, housing allowance (BAH at E-5 rate), and $1,000/year for books. Use it for college, trade school, flight training, on-the-job training, and more. Transfer to dependents is possible.
va.gov/education/about-gi-bill-benefits →
VA Home Loan
No down payment required, no private mortgage insurance, competitive interest rates. One of the most powerful benefits earned through service. Available for purchase, refinance, or adapted housing.
va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans →
Discharge Upgrade
If you received less than honorable discharge and believe it was unjust, you can apply for an upgrade. An upgraded discharge can restore VA benefits worth tens of thousands of dollars.
militaryonesource.mil →
VA Caregiver Support Program
If you care for a veteran with a serious injury or illness, you may qualify for a monthly stipend, health insurance, and mental health services through the Program of Comprehensive Assistance for Fami...
va.gov/family-member-benefits/comprehensive-assistance-for-family-caregivers →

Financial Assistance

Operation Homefront — Emergency Assistance
Provides emergency financial assistance to active duty, Guard, Reserve, and veterans. Helps with rent, utilities, food, auto repair, and more. No repayment required.
operationhomefront.org →
USA Cares
Emergency financial assistance for post-9/11 veterans and military families facing hardship. Helps with rent, mortgage, utilities, and basic needs.
usacares.org →
SNAP (Food Assistance)
Veterans facing food insecurity may qualify for SNAP benefits. Many veterans are surprised to learn they qualify. Apply through your state's social services agency.
fns.usda.gov/snap →
VFW Emergency Loans & Grants
VFW offers interest-free emergency loans and unrepayable grants to veterans in financial crisis through their National Veterans Service program.
vfw.org →
Operation Homefront — Housing
Transitional and permanent housing programs for veterans and military families in need.
operationhomefront.org →

Education & Training

DoD SkillBridge
Work at a civilian company for up to 180 days before ETS while still receiving military pay and benefits. Over 10,000 partner companies including Amazon, Microsoft, and hundreds of trades programs.
skillbridge.defense.gov →
Helmets to Hardhats
Connects veterans to registered apprenticeships in the construction trades — electrical, plumbing, pipefitting, ironwork, and more. Union wages, free training, lifetime career.
helmetstohardhats.org →
MyCAA Scholarship (Military Spouses)
Up to $4,000 in scholarships for military spouses pursuing portable career training, licenses, or degrees.
mycaa.com →
Troops to Teachers
Helps veterans and transitioning service members become certified teachers — covering certification fees and offering bonuses in high-need schools.
proudtoserveagain.com →
💼

Employment & Hiring

Hire Heroes USA
Free career coaching, resume writing, and job placement services for veterans and military spouses. Their coaches are veterans themselves. Average salary for placed veterans: $62,000+.
hireheroesusa.org →
USAJobs.gov — Federal Employment
All federal government jobs. Veterans get 5-10 point preference. Many roles are set aside exclusively for veterans. Create a profile and upload your resume.
usajobs.gov →
RecruitMilitary
Job fairs and online job board specifically for veterans. Top employers attend specifically to hire veterans — Walmart, Home Depot, defense contractors, law enforcement agencies.
recruitmilitary.com →
ClearanceJobs
Job board for cleared professionals. If you hold or held a security clearance, your value in the defense contractor market is significant — often $80K-$150K+ roles.
clearancejobs.com →
American Corporate Partners (ACP)
Free one-on-one mentorship pairing veterans with executives from major corporations. Get career advice, networking, and guidance from senior leaders.
acp-usa.org →

Housing Assistance

HUD-VASH (Veteran Housing Vouchers)
If you are homeless or at risk of homelessness, HUD-VASH provides housing vouchers and VA case management. Call your local VA or 988 (Press 1) to access.
va.gov/homeless →
National Call Center for Homeless Veterans
24/7 hotline connecting homeless or at-risk veterans to housing services, case management, and emergency assistance.
Call 877-4AID-VET →

Legal Assistance

Veterans Legal Services
Free legal help for veterans with VA disability appeals, discharge upgrades, benefits denials, and more. Upgrading a discharge can unlock thousands in back pay and benefits.
veteranslegalservices.org →
National Veterans Legal Services Program
Provides free legal representation for veterans in VA benefit appeals. Has won millions in back benefits for veterans who were wrongly denied.
nvlsp.org →

Family & Caregiver Support

Military OneSource
Free 24/7 support for service members and their families — counseling, financial coaching, tax prep, legal services, relocation assistance, and more. Available up to 365 days after separation.
militaryonesource.mil →

Substance Use & Recovery

SAMHSA National Helpline
Free, confidential, 24/7 treatment referral and information service for substance use disorders. Call 1-800-662-4357.
Call 1-800-662-4357 →
Veteran Recovery Centers
Residential and outpatient recovery programs specifically for veterans. Peer support from fellow veterans in recovery who understand military culture.
va.gov/find-locations/?facilityType=benefits →
