Top 50 Civilian Action Verbs for Veterans

Replace military jargon with these powerful civilian action verbs. Organized by career category so you can find the right word for every bullet on your resume.

Start Free Trial

The most common mistake on veteran resumes: starting bullet points with "Responsible for" or using military terms that ATS systems don't recognize. These 50 action verbs are the foundation of every strong military-to-civilian resume. Use the ones that match your actual duties - then quantify the result.

The Formula

Action Verb + What You Did + Scale + Quantified Result. Example: "Led a 12-person logistics team managing $4.2M in equipment, achieving 99% accountability across an 18-month deployment." Every bullet should follow this structure.

Leadership & Management

Led
Directed a team, unit, or project toward a specific outcome
Commanded
Exercised authority over personnel and resources
Directed
Guided the actions of personnel or operations
Supervised
Oversaw the work and performance of subordinates
Mentored
Developed the skills and career growth of junior personnel
Trained
Built competency in others through instruction and practice
Coordinated
Organized and aligned multiple people or departments
Delegated
Assigned tasks and accountability to team members
Motivated
Inspired personnel to achieve beyond standard expectations
Evaluated
Assessed the performance or quality of personnel or processes

Operations & Execution

Executed
Carried out a plan, mission, or task to completion
Implemented
Put a system, process, or plan into action
Deployed
Positioned resources or personnel for a specific purpose
Managed
Oversaw resources, personnel, or projects to achieve goals
Operated
Used equipment, systems, or processes to produce results
Established
Created or founded a process, program, or system
Launched
Initiated a new program, project, or initiative
Streamlined
Simplified or improved a process to increase efficiency
Maintained
Kept equipment, systems, or standards at required levels
Delivered
Produced or provided a specific output or result

Analysis & Intelligence

Analyzed
Examined data, information, or situations to draw conclusions
Assessed
Evaluated a situation, risk, or capability
Investigated
Conducted a thorough examination to determine facts
Identified
Recognized and documented a problem, opportunity, or threat
Monitored
Continuously tracked data, activity, or performance
Researched
Gathered and synthesized information to support decisions
Evaluated
Judged the value, quality, or effectiveness of something
Forecasted
Projected future outcomes based on data and analysis
Synthesized
Combined information from multiple sources into conclusions
Reported
Documented and communicated findings to decision-makers

Communication & Briefing

Briefed
Communicated information to leadership or stakeholders
Presented
Delivered information formally to an audience
Negotiated
Reached agreements through discussion and compromise
Advised
Provided expert guidance to leaders or teams
Communicated
Exchanged information clearly across teams or organizations
Drafted
Wrote documents, reports, or communications
Authored
Created original written materials or reports
Liaised
Served as the communication link between organizations
Collaborated
Worked jointly with others to achieve shared goals
Facilitated
Guided discussions or processes to productive outcomes

Logistics & Supply Chain

Managed
Oversaw inventory, equipment, or supply chain operations
Procured
Acquired goods or services through formal purchasing processes
Distributed
Allocated resources or materials across an organization
Tracked
Monitored the location or status of assets and personnel
Controlled
Maintained accountability and oversight of property or operations
Planned
Developed detailed approaches to accomplish objectives
Scheduled
Organized timelines and resources for operations
Transported
Moved personnel, equipment, or supplies to required locations
Requisitioned
Formally requested materials, equipment, or services
Audited
Conducted formal reviews of records, inventory, or compliance
