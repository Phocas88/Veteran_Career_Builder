How to Use This List

  • Click any word to copy it to your clipboard instantly.
  • Action verbs should open every bullet point on your resume — never start with "Responsible for" or "Assisted with."
  • Power nouns are the things you did it to. Combine: "Commanded [noun]" → "Commanded 40-person combat logistics team supporting 3 brigade-level operations."
  • Quantify everything. Verbs without numbers are weak. "Managed supply chain" → "Managed $2.4M supply chain for 850-person battalion, maintaining 97% equipment readiness."
  • Match the job posting. Civilian ATS systems scan for specific verbs. Mirror the language in the job description wherever possible.

Using Power Nouns on Your Resume

  • Power nouns replace vague military jargon with civilian-readable descriptions of scope and impact.
  • Combine with a strong verb: "Oversaw multi-echelon operations supporting 3-brigade combat team"
  • Always translate the military term in parentheses — don't assume the recruiter knows what METL means.
  • Add dollar amounts, headcounts, and timeframes wherever possible to give context.
