What the RIASEC Model Means for Veterans

The RIASEC model (Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional) is the most validated career assessment framework in existence. It is used by the Department of Labor, the VA, and career counselors worldwide. What makes ours different is how we apply it.

Realistic (Hands-On and Technical)

Veterans who score high in Realistic typically thrived in technical, mechanical, or physical MOS roles: 91B wheeled vehicle mechanics, 15T helicopter repairers, 12B combat engineers. Civilian paths include skilled trades, manufacturing management, construction, and technical operations.

Investigative (Analytical and Research-Oriented)

High Investigative scores are common among intelligence analysts (35F, 35M, 35N), signal intelligence specialists (35P), and cyber operators (17C, 25D). Civilian paths include intelligence analyst, data scientist, cybersecurity, and program management roles.

Social (People-Oriented and Helping)

Social dimensions are strongest in medics (68W), chaplain assistants (56M), human resources specialists (42A), and those who naturally took on training and mentoring roles. Civilian paths include human resources, healthcare, education, social work, and nonprofit management.

Enterprising (Leadership and Business-Driven)

Veterans who loved being in charge, leading formations, and driving mission results often score highest in Enterprising. This maps to operations management, business development, defense contracting, and executive leadership roles.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from other veteran career assessments?

Most assessments give you generic results. Ours cross-references your RIASEC profile with your actual MOS, rank, clearance level, and military background to give you career recommendations that are specific to you, not just any veteran.

Is the basic test really free?

Yes. The 20-question basic test and your initial career profile are completely free with no account required. The full 60-question deep assessment and detailed career report require a subscription.

How many career matches will I get?

The basic test generates your top career match plus a preview of others. The full paid assessment generates 20 personalized career matches, each with salary ranges, your MOS connection, and a specific next step to take.

Can I take this test if I am still active duty?

Absolutely. Many service members take the assessment during TAP or in the 12 months before their ETS date. Early planning is always better.

Does the test account for my security clearance?

Yes. When you build your profile in the app, you can enter your clearance level. The career recommendations will then include cleared positions that match your background, which often carry a significant salary premium.
