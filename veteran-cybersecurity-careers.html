Cybersecurity Careers for Veterans

Your military background — especially any intelligence, communications, IT, or signals work — is a direct pipeline into one of the fastest-growing and highest-paying civilian career fields.

Translate Your MOS

Cybersecurity is not just for computer science majors. The field rewards discipline, attention to detail, operational thinking under pressure, and the ability to work with classified systems — all core military competencies. Entry-level cybersecurity analysts with a Security+ certification are earning $55,000-$75,000 within 6 months of separation. Senior cleared professionals earn $130,000-$200,000+. The field has a critical shortage of qualified workers — estimates put the gap at 3.5 million unfilled positions globally.

Military Backgrounds That Translate Directly

25D - Cyber Operations Specialist (Army)

Direct translation to SOC analyst, penetration tester, or threat intelligence analyst. Commands a significant salary premium due to rare combination of clearance and technical skills.

Direct Translation
17C - Cyber Operations Officer (Army)

Translates to CISO, security architect, or senior cybersecurity manager. Management experience at scale plus technical depth is extremely rare and well compensated.

Direct Translation
CTN - Cryptologic Technician Networks (Navy)

Network security, signals intelligence, and cyber operations experience translates directly to network security analyst or threat intelligence roles.

Direct Translation
1B4 - Cyberspace Operations (Air Force)

AF cyber operators move into SOC lead, red team, and cloud security architect roles. Often the most technically advanced cyber veterans entering the workforce.

Direct Translation
25B / IT Rate / 3D0 - IT Specialists

Strong foundation for security roles with certifications. CompTIA Security+ is the bridge. Path: Sysadmin → Security Analyst → Security Engineer.

1-2 Certs Needed
35 Series / 1N Series - Intelligence

Threat intelligence and OSINT roles are a natural fit. Analytical tradecraft, reporting skills, and operational context transfer directly.

Career Path Overview

Entry Path (0-12 months after separation): CompTIA A+ if needed → CompTIA Network+ → CompTIA Security+. Apply to SOC Analyst Tier 1 roles at defense contractors or MSSPs. Many DoD cyber roles accept Security+ alone with clearance.

Intermediate (1-3 years): CompTIA CySA+ or CEH → SOC Tier 2 or Security Analyst. Add cloud cert (AWS Security or Azure Security) for cloud-focused roles.

Senior (3-5+ years): CISSP, OSCP, or CCSP depending on specialization. Move to Security Engineer, Penetration Tester, or management track.

Key Certifications

CompTIA Security+

$392 | 2-3 months | DoD 8570 baseline. Required for most federal cyber roles. The single most important cert for transitioning veterans entering cyber.

CompTIA CySA+

$392 | 3-4 months | SOC Tier 2 level. Strong next step after Security+.

CEH (EC-Council)

$950 | 3-6 months | Recognized offensive security credential. Good for penetration testing path.

CISSP (ISC2)

$699 | 6-12 months + 5yr exp | Senior security roles. The gold standard for security leadership.

Top Employers Actively Hiring Veterans

Booz Allen Hamilton

One of the largest employers of cleared veterans. Strong cyber practice. Actively recruits veterans with DoD backgrounds. Strong Veteran Culture

SAIC / Leidos / Peraton

Defense IT contractors. Hundreds of cyber openings at any time. Clearance almost always a requirement. Entry to senior roles available.

NSA / DIA / USCYBERCOM

Federal agencies with active veteran hiring programs. Civilian cyber roles with full federal benefits and veterans preference.

CrowdStrike / Palo Alto Networks

Private sector leaders. Strong veteran hiring programs. Often offer SkillBridge internships.
