💡 Tips for Maximizing Your Military Discounts

  1. Set up ID.me first. One verified account unlocks discounts at hundreds of companies. It takes about 5 minutes.
  2. Always ask. Many local businesses offer military discounts that aren't advertised. It never hurts to ask at the register.
  3. Check before Veterans Day. Plan your route for Veterans Day freebies — many restaurants have limited hours or require dine-in.
  4. Stack discounts carefully. Some military discounts can be combined with sales or coupons — others cannot. Read the fine print.
  5. Get the Veteran designation on your driver's license. Many states let you add "VETERAN" to your state ID, which can serve as proof for in-store discounts.
  6. Sign up for GovX. Their marketplace aggregates exclusive military pricing from hundreds of outdoor, tactical, and lifestyle brands.
  7. Switch your cell phone plan. AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon military plans can save $300-600/year for a family plan.
