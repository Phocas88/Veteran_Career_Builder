🏭 Location Guide

Best Cities for Veteran Careers

Ranked by employment outcomes, not base proximity. The cities where cleared, healthcare, law enforcement, cyber, and logistics veterans find the highest salaries and best career density.

Find My Career Path First

Where you live after separation affects your salary ceiling, hiring network density, clearance job availability, cost of living relative to VA benefits, and quality of life. This guide ranks cities by veteran employment outcomes - not just military base proximity.

How to Read This Guide

Rankings weight: veteran unemployment rate, median salary for veteran-dominant careers, VA facility access, housing cost relative to VA home loan limits, and veteran community density. A city near a base is not automatically veteran-friendly for career growth.

Best Cities for Cleared / Defense / Intelligence Careers

1. Northern Virginia (DC Metro)

Highest density of cleared positions in the world. Arlington, Alexandria, Fairfax County host NSA, DIA, NRO contractors, and most major defense firms. TS/SCI clearance commands $120-160K+ in this market. Expensive cost of living offset by compensation.

Cleared Jobs Intel Community Federal Agencies

2. San Antonio, TX

JBSA is one of the largest military installations in the country. Large veteran population, no state income tax, low cost of living, strong defense contractor presence, and robust VA medical center. Excellent for transitioning infantry, medical, and logistics veterans.

No Income Tax Low Cost of Living Large Veteran Community

3. Colorado Springs, CO

NORAD, US Space Command, Peterson SFB, Fort Carson. Strong cyber and space sector growing rapidly. Mountain lifestyle, relatively affordable compared to Denver, large active and veteran military community.

Space Sector Cyber Growth 5 Military Bases

4. Huntsville, AL

Redstone Arsenal, NASA Marshall, and an extraordinarily dense defense contractor ecosystem. No state income tax on military retirement, very low cost of living, highest concentration of engineers in the country. Underrated for veterans with technical backgrounds.

No Tax on Military Retirement Engineering Hub

Best Cities for Cybersecurity Careers

Northern Virginia / DC Metro

No competition. NSA, Cyber Command, and every major cyber contractor within a 30-mile radius. Average cyber salary $130K+. Cleared cyber professionals are in extreme demand.

Austin, TX

Growing tech hub with expanding cybersecurity sector. No state income tax, lower cost of living than San Francisco or Seattle. Dell, IBM, Apple security teams all based here.

San Antonio, TX

JBSA Lackland hosts NSA Texas and 24th Air Force (cyber). Strong private sector cyber presence following military concentration. Outstanding for veterans exiting cyber MOSs.

Tampa, FL

SOCOM, CENTCOM, USSPECOPS - significant cyber and intel presence. No state income tax. Growing private sector cyber market. Strong veteran population for networking.

Best Cities for Healthcare Careers (Medical MOSs)

Houston, TX

Texas Medical Center is the largest medical complex in the world. 60+ medical institutions, 106,000 employees. 68W and HM veterans find exceptional opportunities. No state income tax, VA Medical Center, lower cost of living than coastal equivalents.

San Antonio, TX

Brooke Army Medical Center (BAMC), large VA system, and strong civilian healthcare market. Military medics and corpsmen are highly sought after. Consistent healthcare job growth.

Phoenix, AZ

Large and growing healthcare market, no cold weather, strong veteran population, and relatively affordable housing. Mayo Clinic, Banner Health, and VA Phoenix all major employers.

Nashville, TN

Healthcare industry capital of the US - more healthcare companies headquartered here per capita than anywhere else. HCA Healthcare, Vanderbilt Health. No state income tax on wages.

States With Zero Income Tax on Military Retirement

For veterans with military retirement pay, these states offer significant financial advantage:

Zero State Income Tax (All Income)

Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington, Wyoming, South Dakota, Tennessee, New Hampshire (on wages)

No Tax on Military Retirement Specifically

Alabama, Arizona, Arkansas, Connecticut, Hawaii, Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Louisiana, Michigan, Mississippi, Missouri, Nebraska, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Wisconsin

Partial Exemptions

California, Oregon, Minnesota, Vermont, and others offer partial exemptions based on age, disability rating, or years of service. Check your specific state.

Cities to Avoid (Despite Large Military Populations)

High cost, lower veteran career density: San Diego and Honolulu have large military populations but extremely high cost of living that erodes VA home loan benefits and starting salaries. Unless you have specific family or career reasons, the financial math is difficult for most transitioning veterans.

Limited private sector depth: Some bases are located near small cities with limited private sector depth (Fort Drum/Watertown NY, Fort Polk/Leesville LA). Veterans at these installations often need to plan relocation for career growth rather than staying in the immediate area.
