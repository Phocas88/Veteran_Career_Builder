Veteran Transition Success Stories

Real career transitions from the veteran community - infantry to PM, medic to PA, IT to cybersecurity, logistics to supply chain. The specific strategies that made each one work.

Names are composites to protect privacy. The career trajectories, timelines, certifications, and strategies described are representative of documented veteran transitions in each field.

Infantry to Project Management

11B/0311 Infantryman - Now: Senior Project Manager, Tech Company

10 Years Service PMP Certified $95K Starting

"Everyone told me I had no transferable skills. I led 40-person platoons through three deployments, managed $2M in equipment, coordinated with six different agencies on every mission - and I had no skills? The problem was I did not know how to talk about it. The PMP gave me a framework and vocabulary civilians understood. I used SkillBridge at Accenture for the last six months of service. Got hired directly out of it."

What worked: SkillBridge fellowship at target company, PMP certification started 12 months before ETS, LinkedIn profile rewritten to focus on scope/schedule/budget management language.

Military Police to Federal Agent

31B Military Police - Now: Special Agent, Federal Agency

6 Years Service TS Clearance Federal LE Career

"I applied to four federal agencies simultaneously - CBP, FBI, NCIS, and DEA. The biggest thing I wish I had known: federal law enforcement hiring takes 12-18 months from application to start date. I filed applications 18 months before my ETS. Most veterans wait until 6 months out and lose a full year of income waiting for the process to clear."

What worked: Applied early (18 months before separation), clearance already active cut months off the process, used veterans preference on every USAJOBS application, took the physical fitness standards seriously well in advance.

Army Medic to Physician Assistant

68W Combat Medic - Now: PA-C, Emergency Medicine

8 Years Service GI Bill + VR&E $130K+ Salary

"I deployed twice as the senior medic for a 200-person battalion. I had more clinical hours than most pre-PA students had ever seen. What I was missing was the prerequisite coursework. I used VR&E (Chapter 31) for my post-bacc prereqs because my separation physical documented a service-connected knee injury. VR&E covered everything GI Bill did not. The PA program specifically recruited veterans - our clinical decision-making under pressure is something they cannot teach in a classroom."

What worked: Filed VR&E claim at separation, completed post-bacc prereqs at community college while working part-time, targeted PA programs with documented veteran-friendly admissions, used deployed clinical hours as significant application differentiator.

Navy IT to Cybersecurity Engineer

IT Navy Rate / 25B Army - Now: Senior Security Engineer, Defense Contractor

6 Years Service TS/SCI $115K Starting

"The clearance was worth six figures on its own. Cleared professionals are so hard to find that defense contractors called me within two days of posting my resume on ClearanceJobs. I had Security+ from the military. I added CEH in the last three months of service using the DoD testing vouchers. Went straight from active duty to a defense contractor at a salary I would not have believed two years before."

What worked: Never let clearance lapse, stacked certifications while still in (Security+, CEH, eventually CISSP), targeted ClearanceJobs and Dice specifically, networked with veterans already at target contractors through LinkedIn veteran ERG connections.

Logistics NCO to Supply Chain Manager

92A/88M Army Logistics - Now: Supply Chain Manager, Fortune 500

12 Years Service APICS CSCP Promoted Twice in 3 Years

"My resume said I was a '92 Alpha.' The hiring manager had no idea what that meant. When I rewrote it to say 'Inventory Control Manager - Managed $14M in equipment and supplies for 800-person organization with zero inventory losses over 18 months,' I started getting calls. The APICS certification helped civilian supply chain teams understand that I spoke their language. I was promoted to manager within 14 months."

What worked: Complete civilian translation of every resume bullet, APICS CSCP certification, targeted companies with formal veteran hiring programs (Amazon, Walmart, Lockheed), highlighted ERP system experience (GCSS-Army translates to SAP experience).

Intelligence Analyst to Private Sector Intelligence

35F/0231 Intelligence Analyst - Now: Corporate Intelligence Analyst, Financial Firm

7 Years Service TS/SCI CI Poly $110K Starting

"Financial firms, law firms, consulting companies, and tech companies all need intelligence professionals now. It is called 'competitive intelligence,' 'threat intelligence,' or 'geopolitical risk analysis' depending on the company. Same skills. I networked into a financial firm through a veteran who had made the same transition two years earlier. He told me what to search for and what language to use. That 20-minute call was worth more than six months of applications."

What worked: Identified civilian vocabulary equivalents (OSINT, threat analysis, geopolitical risk), networked with veterans who had made the exact same transition, targeted financial services and consulting specifically for highest-paying roles, cleared status opened additional doors.

Aviation Mechanic to Defense Contractor / Airline

15T/6113 Aviation Mechanic - Now: A&P Mechanic, Major Airline

6 Years Service FAA A&P License $85K Starting + Benefits

"The FAA A&P is the key. Military aviation experience counts toward the experience requirements - you do not have to start over. I got both A&P certificates (Airframe and Powerplant) before I separated. The airlines are actively recruiting veterans and the pay progression is very good. I was making six figures within four years."

What worked: Obtained FAA A&P certification before separation, targeted airline veteran hiring programs directly (most majors have them), used military aviation maintenance logs as documentation for FAA experience requirements.

Common Patterns Across All Successful Transitions

Started Planning 12-18 Months Out

Every successful transition in this collection started networking, applying, or certifying well before ETS. The veterans who struggled waited until 90 days out.

Got One Civilian Credential

PMP, Security+, APICS, A&P, NREMT-P - one industry-recognized certification gave civilian hiring managers a reference point for military experience they could not otherwise evaluate.

Found One Insider Contact

Every story here includes a veteran who had made the same transition and spent 20-30 minutes giving specific advice. This one conversation consistently moved the timeline up by months.

Used Available Programs

SkillBridge, VR&E, GI Bill, Hiring Our Heroes, veteran ERGs, DOD testing vouchers - the programs that accelerated these transitions were all free. Most veterans do not use them.
