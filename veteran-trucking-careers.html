Trucking Careers for Veterans

Real CDL programs, company-paid training, and veteran hiring pipelines. Get your CDL in 3-7 weeks — many programs are free for veterans through GI Bill, VR&E, or company sponsorship.

See CDL Programs for Veterans ↓

Trucking is one of the fastest paths from separation to a $70,000+ income. The CDL can be earned in 3-7 weeks, and veterans with military driving experience often test out of significant portions of training. This guide has real links to real programs — GI Bill-approved CDL schools, company-paid training with application links, and veteran-specific hiring pipelines.

Military Backgrounds That Translate

88M - Motor Transport Operator (Army)

Direct CDL translation. 88Ms with HEMTT/LMTV/PLS experience often skip the driving skills portion of CDL testing. Many states have military CDL skills test waivers — check yours.

Direct Translation
3E2 (AF) / BU Seabee (Navy) / 1345 (USMC)

Heavy equipment operators can add CDL-A quickly. Strong pipeline into construction logistics, heavy haul, and specialized freight. Your VMET or DD-214 may show qualifying vehicle operations.

Strong Foundation
Any MOS with Military Driving Hours

Any service member who operated wheeled vehicles (tactical or otherwise) likely has more drive time than civilian CDL applicants. The FMCSA military CDL waiver lets you skip the skills test in many states if you had a military license within the last year.

Good Foundation
FMCSA Military CDL Waiver

Federal law allows states to waive the CDL skills test for veterans with qualifying military driving experience. You still need to pass the written knowledge test. Check your state's requirements at fmcsa.dot.gov/military ↗

Free & GI Bill CDL Training Programs

These are real programs with current application links. GI Bill-approved programs pay tuition + a monthly housing allowance while you train.

FASTPORT — Troops into Transportation

The largest veteran-to-trucking pipeline. Partners with 200+ carriers and CDL schools. Free career matching, apprenticeship placement, and CDL training connections. Start here.

Free · No Cost Top Resource
Registered CDL Apprenticeships (DOL)

Department of Labor registered apprenticeships — earn while you learn. Many qualify for GI Bill benefits. Search by state at apprenticeship.gov/finder.

Earn While Training
GI Bill Approved CDL Schools

Search VA-approved CDL programs in your state. GI Bill covers tuition + monthly housing allowance (E-5 BAH rate). Use the GI Bill Comparison Tool ↗ to find approved schools near you.

GI Bill Covered
VR&E (Chapter 31) CDL Training

If you have a service-connected disability rating, VR&E can pay for your entire CDL program plus tools, supplies, and a monthly subsistence allowance. Often faster approval than GI Bill for CDL.

Disability Rating Required
DOL Veterans Employment Programs

The Department of Labor's VETS program funds job training for veterans through state workforce agencies. Contact your local American Job Center ↗ — many offer funded CDL training.

Free Through Workforce
SBA Veteran Business Resources

Planning to go owner-operator? SBA has veteran-specific microloans and business development programs. Find your local SBA office ↗ for free business planning help.

Owner-Operator Path

Company-Paid CDL Training (No Upfront Cost)

These companies pay for your CDL training in exchange for a 1-2 year driving commitment. All have veteran-specific hiring programs.

Werner Enterprises — Veterans Program

Company-paid CDL training. Dedicated military recruiting team. $10,000 sign-on bonus for experienced CDL holders. Apprenticeship program registered with VA for GI Bill benefits.

Free CDL Training $10K Bonus
Schneider National — Military Program

Company-paid CDL training (Schneider covers 100%). Military Apprenticeship Program (earn GI Bill BAH while training). Guaranteed home weekly on many routes. Top-rated veteran employer.

Free CDL Training GI Bill Compatible
Swift Transportation — Veterans

Paid CDL training program (3 weeks). Mentorship program for new drivers. One of the largest fleets in North America. Tuition reimbursement available.

Free CDL Training
C.R. England — Military Division

Company-sponsored CDL school. Dedicated military recruiter. Guaranteed job placement upon completion. Regional and OTR opportunities.

Free CDL Training
Roehl Transport — Veterans

Get Your CDL Program — Roehl pays 100%. No contract payback if you stay 1 year. Flatbed, van, and refrigerated divisions. Strong safety culture.

Free CDL Training

Specialized Endorsements That Boost Pay

Hazmat (H) Endorsement

+$5,000-$15,000/year. Requires TSA background check and written test. Veterans with military hazmat/CBRN experience have an advantage. TSA Hazmat info ↗

+$10K/yr avg
Tanker (N) Endorsement

+$5,000-$10,000/year. Required for liquid/gas transport. Written test only. Combined with Hazmat as "X" endorsement for maximum pay.

+$7K/yr avg
Doubles/Triples (T) Endorsement

+$3,000-$8,000/year. Common in LTL freight (UPS, FedEx, Old Dominion). Written test only. Good path to higher-paying LTL positions.

+$5K/yr avg

Your Next Steps

Week-by-Week Action Plan
  1. This week: Check your state's military CDL waiver eligibility. Get your medical card (DOT physical, ~$100).
  2. Week 2: Register at FASTPORT for free career matching. Search GI Bill-approved CDL schools in your area.
  3. Week 3: Apply to 2-3 company-paid programs (Werner, Schneider, Swift) OR enroll in a GI Bill CDL school.
  4. Weeks 4-7: Complete CDL training (3-7 weeks depending on program).
  5. Month 3: Start driving. Build 6-12 months experience, then apply to premium carriers (Walmart, UPS, FedEx Freight).
